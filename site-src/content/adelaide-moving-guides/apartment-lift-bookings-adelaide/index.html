Before quote review

What to lock in before the apartment move is priced.

Apartment moves often look smaller than they really are because the building controls the route. The lift booking, dock location, and corridor rules determine how quickly the crew can stage the load. That is why apartment planning should sit next to the residential service page and not be treated like an afterthought once the date is already booked.

01

Confirm the service-lift window

Know the booking time, how long the lift is reserved, and whether the move can overrun if loading starts late.

02

Check dock and truck access

Apartment buildings often have a nominated loading point, a contractor entrance, or a permit process that changes where the truck can stand.

03

Stage the fragile and priority items first

When the building access is tight, packed cartons, electronics, and delicate pieces should already be separated so the lift time is not wasted repacking on site.

A stronger apartment brief includes

These details usually keep the quote more reliable.

  • Building name, level, lift booking time, and whether a dock is required
  • Parking setup, corridor protection rules, and key or concierge requirements
  • Bulky furniture, whitegoods, fragile items, and whether packing is still incomplete
  • Pickup and delivery property types so both buildings are scoped properly
