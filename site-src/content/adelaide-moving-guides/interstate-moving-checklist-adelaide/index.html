Checklist

The interstate checklist Adelaide clients should work through first.

The checklist below is built for real route planning, not generic moving advice. If you work through these points before you request a proposal, you are far more likely to receive a useful scope review instead of a vague estimate that needs to be reworked later.

01

Confirm both addresses and property types

House, apartment, townhouse, office, or mixed-use conditions at both ends should be confirmed before the move is scoped.

02

List access limits at pickup and delivery

Lift bookings, stairs, docks, loading zones, parking limits, and longer carries all need to be known before the proposal is finalised.

03

Build a usable inventory

Heavy furniture, whitegoods, fragile items, storage stock, and unusual pieces should be listed clearly enough to support a real plan.

04

Decide what needs packing or extra protection

Long routes reward better wrapping, carton planning, and staging. This is where packing support often becomes valuable.

05

Lock in timing commitments

Settlement, building access windows, delivery deadlines, and key handovers should be explained early so the route can be staged properly.

06

Flag anything that could interrupt delivery

Delayed settlement, late keys, after-hours building rules, or storage contingencies all change how much risk needs to be managed.

Why it matters

The best interstate moves are decided before move day.

Interstate jobs are less forgiving than local ones. If there is confusion around access, packing depth, or handover timing, the consequences are bigger because the route is longer and there is less room to improvise. That is why a stronger pre-move brief matters so much.

Interstate mistakes

What usually creates avoidable friction on a long route.

The most common issue is not distance by itself. It is incomplete information. Clients sometimes know the destination city but not the delivery access, or they know the settlement date but not the actual handover window. Others underestimate how much extra protection certain items need when they are in transit for longer. Those gaps are usually what create pricing changes or schedule pressure later.

Interstate FAQ

Questions people ask before interstate moves from Adelaide.

When should I start planning an interstate move from Adelaide?

As early as possible, especially if the move has apartment access, settlement dates, delivery windows, or packing requirements at either end.

Why do both properties matter on an interstate quote?

Because lifts, stairs, loading zones, parking, and carry distance at both pickup and delivery points all affect the labour plan and handover timing.

Does interstate packing need to be more detailed than a local move?

Usually yes. Longer transit windows make protection, carton quality, wrapping, and load sequencing more important for fragile or higher-value items.

What detail helps an interstate proposal stay accurate?

Origin, destination, property types, move date, timing windows, access limits, heavy items, packing scope, and any settlement or key-handover commitments.
