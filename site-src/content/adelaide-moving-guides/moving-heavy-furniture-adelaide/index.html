The planning sequence

Start with the path, then the furniture, then the labour.

People often start by listing the item. That is useful, but it is not enough. The better sequence is to confirm the pickup and delivery path, identify any stairs, turns, service lifts, or sloped access, then decide whether the piece should be wrapped, dismantled, or moved as-is. On some routes, especially coastal apartments or CBD buildings, the access conditions matter more than the size of the item itself.

01

Map the pickup and delivery path

Note stairs, lifts, narrow turns, garage entries, verandas, and any point where the item cannot travel in a straight line.

02

Decide what should be dismantled

Beds, large tables, and shelving often move more safely when the item is reduced before the carry starts.

03

Separate delicate pieces from standard bulk

Stone tops, mirrors, glass cabinets, and polished finishes need a stronger protection brief than standard timber furniture.
