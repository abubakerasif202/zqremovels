# Local Service Landing Pages

Use this guide for moving, trades, clinics, legal, or other local-service businesses.

## Pick Intent Clearly

- Give each page one primary phrase such as `removalists glenelg` or `interstate removalists adelaide`.
- Support it with a small set of secondary phrases, not a long keyword dump.
- Avoid creating multiple pages for the same intent unless they serve genuinely different users.

## Write Unique Substance

Every location or service page should have unique material in at least these areas:

- The heading and intro.
- Service-area details or operational constraints.
- Proof points, trust signals, or route-specific considerations.
- Internal links to adjacent relevant pages.
- Calls to action that reflect the page intent.

If a page only swaps suburb names, treat it as thin and rewrite it.

## Keep The Structure Simple

- Title: primary phrase plus brand when it still reads naturally.
- H1: plain-language version of the promise.
- Intro: what is offered, where, and why this business is credible.
- Body sections: scope, process, coverage, pricing approach, FAQs, trust signals.
- CTA: one primary conversion action and one fallback contact action.

## Match Schema To Content

- Keep business identity schema on the site.
- Add `Service` schema to location or service pages when the service is explicitly described there.
- Keep service areas and phone numbers consistent between schema and page copy.

## Internal Linking

- Link from the home page to priority service and suburb pages.
- Link laterally between closely related suburbs or service types only when the link helps the user.
- Use descriptive anchors, not repeated `click here` style labels.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent reviews, ratings, awards, or office addresses.
- Do not add FAQ schema for hidden or nonexistent FAQ content.
- Do not promise city coverage that operations cannot support.
