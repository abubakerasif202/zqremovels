Packing plan

Materials and protection matched to the move.

Packing is most useful when it reflects what is actually moving. Fragile kitchens, artwork, mirrors, electronics, wardrobes, and office equipment need a different approach from everyday cartons.

Everyday packing done clearly

Room-by-room packing keeps the move easier to load, unload, and settle into at the new property.

  • Kitchens, wardrobes, bedrooms, and living areas
  • Room labels that carry through the move
  • Useful for local homes and apartments

Extra care for fragile items

Mirrors, artwork, glassware, delicate furniture, and other fragile items benefit from a clearer packing plan before the move.

  • Fragile pieces flagged in the quote brief
  • Protection matched to the item and route
  • Helpful for longer interstate travel as well
Service levels

Choose the level of packing help that fits the move.

Some people only want the fragile rooms handled. Others want most of the property prepared before move day. The quote can be shaped around either option.

Option 01

Targeted fragile packing

Ideal when you can handle the basics yourself but want help with the rooms or items most likely to break or slow the move down.

  • Kitchens, mirrors, artwork, and breakables
  • Large or delicate furniture touchpoints
  • Good for shorter local moves or single rooms
Option 02

Most-of-home packing

Useful when you want the bulk of the packing done before move day without leaving the whole property to the last minute.

  • Bedrooms, living areas, kitchens, and wardrobes
  • Cleaner loading flow on moving day
  • Helpful for family homes and apartments
Option 03

Full packing support

For moves where you want the property prepared professionally before the removal team arrives.

  • Whole-home or fuller office preparation
  • Less last-minute rush before handover
  • Useful on interstate routes and tighter deadlines
Move-day benefit

Good packing makes the move day easier.

The better the packing brief, the easier it is to load, protect, unload, and place the items in the right rooms at the other end.

Packing becomes even more important on longer routes. If your move is leaving South Australia, start with our interstate removals hub so the packing brief and the delivery plan can be scoped together.

Room-by-room labels and placement

When boxes are labelled properly, the unload is easier to direct and the property is simpler to settle into.

  • Priority rooms can be identified early
  • Boxes matched to the destination rooms
  • Less confusion during unloading

Better protection for longer travel

Packing standards matter more when the load is staying on the road longer or moving through tighter delivery windows.

  • Fragile items protected before loading
  • Longer routes handled with more preparation
  • Interstate packing can be included in the quote

Packing FAQ

Common questions before booking packing help.

These answers cover the decisions people usually want to make before adding packing support to a move.

Do I need full packing to use the service?

No. Many customers only book packing for fragile items, the kitchen, wardrobes, or the rooms that would take the most time on their own.

Can packing be combined with an interstate move?

Yes. Longer routes usually benefit from a stronger packing standard because the items are travelling further and need to stay protected for longer.

What should I include in the packing quote request?

Include the property type, the move date, the rooms you want packed, and any fragile or bulky items that need extra care.
