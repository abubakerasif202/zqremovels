Protection first

Delicate finishes and bulky pieces need the right handling plan.

Polished timber, glass panels, mirrors, stone tops, upholstered furniture, and large sofas all need a clearer plan than a quick blanket wrap on the day.

Protection matched to the item

Different finishes and shapes need different levels of wrapping, padding, and loading care.

  • Edges and corners protected early
  • Glass and delicate surfaces flagged in the quote brief
  • Furniture grouped by handling needs

Access checked before moving day

The path out of the property matters almost as much as the item itself when the furniture is large or awkward.

  • Stairs, corridors, lifts, and tight turns reviewed early
  • Measurements or photos can help confirm the plan
  • Packing support available for delicate pieces
What we commonly move

Furniture that usually needs extra planning.

The most common requests are not unusual items. They are the everyday pieces that become difficult because they are large, delicate, or awkward to move through the property.

Large household furniture

Lounges, dining tables, bed frames, cabinets, entertainment units, and other bulky furniture often need a clearer lifting and loading plan.

  • Weight and size considered before the move
  • Placement order worked out early
  • Useful for local and interstate routes

Delicate timber, glass, and statement pieces

Furniture with fragile finishes or more complex shapes benefits from slower handling and better protection before it is loaded.

  • Glass panels and mirrors flagged in the brief
  • Delicate finishes wrapped properly
  • Fragile pieces separated from the main load where needed

Awkward access

Tight stairs, lifts, and difficult access reviewed before the quote is sent.

The job changes when the furniture has to move through narrow hallways, shared apartment entries, stairwells, or tighter loading positions. That access needs to be part of the plan from the start.

Access step 01

Review the path out

Confirm stairs, lifts, corners, balcony access, parking position, and whether the item can travel safely through the normal route.

  • Photos or measurements help on tighter jobs
  • Building-management rules can be factored in early
  • Large or awkward items scoped separately
Access step 02

Protect and stage the item

Once the path is clear, the furniture can be wrapped and staged for the safest carry and loading sequence.

  • Extra padding for doorframes and tighter turns
  • Longer carries allowed for in the quote
  • Placement order planned before loading
Access step 03

Place it properly at the destination

Difficult access matters at the new property as well, so the delivery side needs the same level of attention.

  • Destination access checked before arrival
  • Room placement matched to the brief
  • Interstate coordination available if needed
Furniture FAQ

Questions customers ask before booking a furniture move.

These answers focus on handling, access, and what needs to be included in the quote request.

What types of furniture do you move in Adelaide?

We move all household and office furniture including sofas, beds, bed frames, dining tables, wardrobes, tall boys, bookshelves, TV units, desks, pianos, antiques, mirrors, and artwork.

Do you move pianos?

Yes. Pianos require specialist handling due to their weight and mechanics. We use appropriate equipment and experienced crew. Please mention piano moves when requesting your quote.

How do you protect furniture during the move?

Every piece of furniture is wrapped in heavy-duty furniture blankets before loading. Fragile items receive additional protection with foam wrapping or cardboard corner guards. Furniture is secured in the truck to prevent movement in transit.

Can I hire you to move just one or two pieces of furniture?

Yes. We offer single-item and small furniture moves. Many Adelaide customers hire us to move just a fridge, sofa, or dining table between addresses.

How much does furniture removal cost in Adelaide?

Furniture removals start from $150 per hour for a two-person team. Single-item moves have a minimum charge. Cost varies by volume, access, and distance. Get a free quote for your specific job.

Are my furniture items insured during the move?

Yes. ZQ Removals is fully insured. Your furniture is covered from the moment our team begins loading to the moment everything is placed in your new home.
