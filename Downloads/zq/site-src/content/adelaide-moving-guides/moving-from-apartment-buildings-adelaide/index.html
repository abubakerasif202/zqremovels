Before the booking is final

Confirm the building rules first, then build the move around them.

Apartment moves fail when the building controls are treated as an afterthought. The important questions are usually operational rather than residential: which lift can be used, whether the dock is booked, how close the truck can stand, whether the building needs corridor protection, and what happens if the crew arrives outside the approved window. Those are the details that change the proposal and the move-day pace.

Apartment move FAQ

Questions people ask before moving out of Adelaide apartment buildings.

What usually causes the most friction on an apartment move?

Booked lift windows, loading restrictions, building rules, and unclear parking arrangements are the biggest sources of friction.

Do smaller apartment moves still need a detailed plan?

Yes. Apartment moves can be operationally tight even when the inventory is modest because the building controls the access window.

What should I ask building management before move day?

Ask about service lifts, loading docks, after-hours access, protective requirements, parking rules, and whether a booking confirmation is needed.

Does apartment packing need to be different?

Usually yes. Tighter entries, stacked loads, and longer carries make labelling, carton strength, and fragile-item protection more important.
